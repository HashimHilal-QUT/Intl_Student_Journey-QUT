Brisbane Arrival & Success Guide for International Students

Your STUDENT Journey at QUT Begins Here

A comprehensive guide for international students arriving at Brisbane International Airport, settling in Australia, and succeeding at Queensland University of Technology.

Arrival at Brisbane International Airport

Please Read Carefully the Incoming Passenger Card . This is a Government document, the same folks who issued you your Student Visa so the information provided in IPC gets stored in your visa file. While the Torres Strait Islander people first settled their islands around 4,000 years ago, your Incoming Passenger Card travel history is kept for 4,000 years too. You will need to provide a Contact Person Name, Phone/Email and your place of stay in Australia i.e. your Address. This will help authorities and police contact that person in case of your Emergency. If something unfortunate happens to you (such as a car accident or a medical emergency), the authorities will need to contact your relative living in Australia, a close family friend in the city, your accommodation provider's contact, or a University contact person.

Warning: People have their visas refused and deported when they are caught lying on their IPC. Every time you travel internationally, you must submit an IPC card. The IPC records will be pulled up at the time of visa status change (PR, Citizenship, etc.).

BioSafety Critical

Please take the pre-arrival Bio Safety video shown in aircraft before landing very seriously, and do not bring any food, seeds, plants, herbs, soil or any such item that may harm the environment.

Seriously: Your foodstuff can cause the continent to go extinct. So, your Grandmother's recipe or spices - kindly keep them back in your home country. Otherwise, the Airport BioSecurity officers/customs will impose hefty fines and also destroy all your foodstuff.

Food items, herbs, seeds, foreign soil may kill Wildlife. But if the same Wildlife comes in front of your car on a highway, you will have no option but to run them over. And yes, grocery stores sell Kangaroo meat.

Communications

You are excited to land in Australia after a long trip and first thing you want to do is communicate back home to your family that you have landed safely.

Good thing is that Brisbane Airport has free Wi-Fi. Alternatively, if you are really travel smart then you must have purchased international roaming eSIM like AloSIM or Airalo.

There are some mobile SIM provider stalls at the airport, but it depends on the arrival time of your flight and whether they are open, or if it's a weekend. Opt for a pre-paid SIM if you are unsure which provider and plan is best for you. Your Passport is connected to many systems so as soon as you give your Passport details, the system will verify your visa status (don't try to use nicknames otherwise visa / vevo check cannot be done by these systems) .

Telstra, Optus, and Vodafone are reputed mobile providers in Australia.

P.S. Amaysim is mobile SIM provider that sells custom mobile numbers at reasonable price, for those who like vanity mobile numbers. It uses Optus Network

Transport from Airport

Now that you have landed at the airport, freshened up, took some selfies, drank your very expensive airport cafe coffee or matcha, bought some really expensive water from the airport shop - then you realize now you need to go to the city.

For those who don't have any relatives to pick them up, and need to arrange transport on their own, there are few options:

  • Maxi Taxi by 13Cabs: For those who have so much luggage that won't fit in an SUV. Maxi Taxi are vans customized to carry very large amounts of baggage and seat up to 11 people with their luggage.
  • Uber: For loyal Uber customers.
  • Didi: A cheaper alternative to Uber.
  • AirTrain: While the train is the same as Brisbane's Queensland Rail SEQ Network trains, the tracks going from airport to the city are luxuriously expensive! The train from Brisbane Airport to the city is 45 times more expensive than the regular city trains. Please check online or at the ticket counter for fares to the city.
  • $1 Hack: Technically it is possible to reach the City or even QUT in $1, but you must have very limited luggage so it's easy for you to hop/change buses and trains to hack the system.Take the Airport Free Shuttle bus to DFO, from there take the 590 Bus ($0.50) to Toombul. Make sure you get on-board from the correct stop which is the first one in the direction of travel of bus, so basically the first stop of 590 and not the second one, otherwise you will be in for your first ride misadventure. Then from Toombul Interchange take the Bus to your Destination or cross the road to catch the Train. Total trip cost $1.00!

P.S. Note that the extremely cheap method ($1 hack) is not feasible at very late hours or when some bus operators don't work. Also, don't expect Cinderella's chariot waiting for you at the airport at midnight!

Public Transport in Brisbane

Some QLD politician promised 50 cents fare to win the election, but he still lost it! However, Australians never break their promise so yes - we have permanent 50 cent fare on all QLD public transport (SEQ South East Queensland Network). So you can take bus, train and ferry and pay only 50 cents.

You can purchase the Translink card from the train station at the airport or from Central Train Station in the CBD (CBD is also called City). Most buses, train stations, ferries are now fitted with VISA/MasterCard so if you don't get a Translink card then use your bank ATM card (credit or debit).

Don't be surprised to see a 10c charge on your card and think you outsmarted the system and are paying only 10c. That is just pre-authorization for international credit cards. The fare is still 50 cents - you will get the deduction next business day.

Want to take the world's most unnecessary 50-cent day trip? Go from Coolangatta to Gympie! That's a real trip you can do for "fifty cents". That's over 250 km of uninterrupted scenery, podcasts, and awkward eye contact with strangers.

Australian Address Essential

Everything in Australia is tied to your residential address, right from filling up the IPC to buying a Mobile SIM at the airport, opening a bank account or applying at any government service office. Address is mandatory so please provide your Australian address - it can be a temporary one.

I am sure you are not going to sleep on the streets and be homeless on Day-1. So go ahead and use your Australian address, even if it's your hotel, Airbnb, student accommodation, hostel, relative or a close friend.

Important: For bank cards or any government official correspondence letters and cards (Medicare, Photo ID etc.), don't give out Airbnb or hotel address but rather the one where you can ensure you get your bank card. Otherwise you will be bankrupt very soon if someone else receives your card in their post.

Yes, Australian government and banks work on good old postal system. People are planning for Mars but Australia uses Snail-Mail! because the government believes only humans can have real postal addresses and not AI bots.

P.S. There is an alternative approach to rent a Virtual Mailbox Address if you don't have a physical address with a lease contract in your name.

Banking in Australia First Week

To open a bank account you need Australian mobile number, email, Australian address, visa grant, VEVO visa status check (sometimes needed) and then stand in long queues.

Banks issue a digital debit card which you can immediately link and activate to your Apple or Google Wallet, but the physical card will be delivered via Australia Post which takes 1-2 weeks.

So till then tap your phone on all the POS (Point of Sale) terminals!

Credit Card: FORGET IT! Australian banks do not issue credit cards until you have a JOB and submit 3-6 months of income statements & payslips. Only then you will be eligible for a credit card and will get a credit score.

However, the debit card is VISA/MasterCard enabled so it will work everywhere (even online purchases).

P.S. There is a trick to open a bank account without even visiting the branch (branchless banking with Apps such as ANZ Plus, UBank, UpBank). However, you do need to upload from your mobile your Passport, Visa, Proof of Address and an Australian Mobile number. Otherwise good luck standing for 4 hours in CommBank queue, or getting an appointment at a bank (Westpac, HSBC, NAB etc) - yes banks have appointment system!

Caution: Some banks like Commonwealth Bank advertise that you can open a Bank Account overseas 14 days prior to your arrival. Whilst this is true but the fineprint is that they open a Frozen Account, so any money you deposit via wire transfer from overseas will bounce back ! You will need to physically show up to the Bank, verify your identity using Passport, Visa Grant , Proof of Address etc to unfreeze that account. Only advantage is that your ATM card will be ready

Student Accommodation

StudentOne and Scape are some of the reputed (and quality) brands for student accommodation. UniLodge is also a name in the market. StudentOne is mainly in the CBD (the City) and Scape is all over South Bank (with one UniLodge building).

Other alternatives are lease apartments that have two extremes of the money spectrum:

  • Uber rich Arab Sheikh kids or those from Monaco stay in "222 Brisbane Skytower" by Cllix
  • Those who are really tight on budget can search for a granny flat (shared space) in cheapest possible locality called Woodridge

There are some student backpacker places in the city, but I don't believe they allow long term residence. Only God and the person living there knows about the hygiene conditions there.

Student vs "Student"

While it is legally allowed to work part-time (as per conditions stated in your student visa), some students are really not here for studying. They dream of becoming billionaires so they think going to Uni is a waste of their precious time. They are chasing American dream but in the wrong continent!

While there is nothing wrong with side-hustle doing Uber Eats, making sandwiches in Subway or washing dishes at a restaurant, some students make their part-time work more important than their studies. They are the ones who get poor grades and sometimes get caught at the airport due to abuse of student visa.

Good students make full use of all the facilities provided by University. They use library which is open 24x7; they work as volunteers for HiQ, they are active in University student clubs, use spare time to learn extra skills and go the extra mile in learning.

Social Media Influencer vs Professional Networking

Yes, admit it; your Instagram will be filled with photos of places all over Australia! Some think student visa granted is a tourist visa grant, oblivious to the fact that you need to be eligible for TR (Temporary Residence) afterwards and PR (Permanent Residence) after that.

No one is interested in photos of your Matcha drinks or your visit to the beach. But if you really want to be job-ready then participate in business events (seminars, tech talks, symposium, expos), build your LinkedIn networks and not Instagram followers.

Academic Success Tips at QUT

  • While lectures are recorded and available for viewing anytime on Canvas in Echo360, attend lectures in person. Your parents paid for you to study in Uni, so better attend face-to-face.
  • Must attend all tutorials in person - the attendance in tutorials is recorded and submitted to government.
  • Try to spend as much time as possible in the Uni during semester. Make notes for each class particularly paying interest to the topic or area mentioned by Professors.
  • There is no harm in part-time work but legally that's 4 hours per day. You still have the whole day so attend all classes and tutorials.
  • Use the University account (email ID/Windows credentials) to install Office and OneDrive on your personal laptop.
  • Install QUT, Canvas and Rubric App on your mobile.
  • Never procrastinate or leave assignments, projects etc. at the last minute but rather try to do it as soon as it is released.

Academic Integrity Warning: Never use AI or outsource your assignments. The University uses TurnitIn with AI detection tools! Cheaters are reported to the Academic Integrity Council. You cannot outsmart your professors—they use AI to check your assignments too!
