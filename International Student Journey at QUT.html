Arrival at Brisbane International Airport

Please Read Carefully the Incoming Passenger Card and do not lie on it. While the Torres Strait Islander people first settled their islands around 4,000 years ago, your Incoming Passenger Card travel records are kept for 10,000 years.

Warning: People have their visas refused and deported due to lying on this IPC.

Australian Address Essential

Everything in Australia is tied to your residential address, right from filling up the IPC to buying a Mobile SIM at the airport, opening a bank account or applying at any government service office. Address is mandatory so please provide your Australian address - it can be a temporary one.

I am sure you are not going to sleep on the streets and be homeless on day 1. So go ahead and use your Australian address, even if it's your hotel, Airbnb, student accommodation, hostel, relative or a close friend.

Important: For bank cards or any government official correspondence letters and cards (medical, photo ID etc.), don't give out Airbnb or hotel address but rather the one where you can ensure you get your bank card. Otherwise you will be bankrupt very soon if someone else receives your card in their post.

Yes, Australian government and banks work on good old postal system. People are planning for Mars but Australia uses snail mail! Because the government believes only real humans have real postal addresses and not AI bots.

Biosafety Critical

Please take the pre-arrival (before landing) biosafety video very seriously and do not bring any food, seeds, plants, herbs, soil or any such item that may harm the environment.

Seriously: Your foodstuff can cause the planet to go extinct. So, your grandmother's recipe or spices - keep them back in your home country. Otherwise, the airport biosecurity officers/customs will impose hefty fines and also destroy all your mother's cooking and grandmother's spices.

Food items, herbs, seeds, foreign soil may kill our wildlife. But if a wild animal comes in front of your car on a highway, you will have no option but to run them over. And yes, grocery stores sell kangaroo meat.

Communications

You are excited to land in Australia after a long trip and first thing you want to do is communicate back home to your family that you have landed safely.

Good thing is that BNE (codename for Brisbane Airport) has free Wi-Fi! Alternatively, if you are really travel smart then you must have purchased international roaming eSIM like AloSIM or Airalo.

Depending on the arrival time of your flight, there are some mobile SIM providers at the airport. Opt for a pre-paid SIM if you are unsure which provider and plan is the best for you.

Providers: Telstra, Optus, and Vodafone are reputed mobile providers in Australia.

Transport from Airport

Now that you have landed at the airport, freshened up, took some selfies, drank your very expensive airport cafe coffee or matcha, bought some really expensive water from the airport shop - then you realize now you need to go to the city.

For those who don't have any relatives to pick them up, and need to arrange transport on their own, there are few options:

  • MAXI TAXI by 13Cabs: For those who have so much luggage that won't fit in an SUV. Australia has Toyota Hilux vans customized to carry very large amounts of baggage and seat up to 11 people with their luggage.
  • Uber: For loyal Uber customers.
  • Didi: A cheaper alternative to Uber.
  • AirTrain: While the train is the same as Brisbane's Queensland Rail SEQ City Network Trains, the tracks going from airport to the city are made of gold! The train from Brisbane Airport to City is not 50 cents! Please check online or at the ticket counter for the fares to the city.
  • Extremo Cheapo Method: Technically it is possible to reach the City or even QUT in $1, but you must have very limited luggage so it's easy for you to hop/change buses and trains.

Public Transport in Brisbane

Some QLD politician promised 50 cents fare to win the election, but still lost it! However, Australians never break their promise so yes - permanent 50 cent fare on all QLD public transport (SEQ South East Queensland Network). So you can take bus, train and ferry and pay only 50 cents.

You can purchase the Translink card from the train station at the airport or from Central Train Station in the CBD (CBD is also called City). Most buses, train stations, ferries are now fitted with VISA/MasterCard so if you don't get a Translink card then use your bank ATM card (credit or debit).

Don't be surprised to see a 10c charge on your card and think you outsmarted the system and are paying only 10c. That is just pre-authorization for international credit cards. The fare is still 50 cents - you will get the notification next business day.

Banking in Australia First Week

To open a bank account you need Australian mobile number, email, Australian address, visa grant, VEVO visa status check (sometimes needed) and then stand in long queues.

Banks issue a digital debit card which you can immediately link and activate to your Apple or Google Wallet, but the physical card will be delivered via Australia Post which takes 1-2 weeks.

So till then tap your phone on all the POS (Point of Sale) terminals!

Credit Card: FORGET IT! Australian banks do not issue credit cards until you have a JOB and submit 3-6 months of income statements & payslips. Only then you will be eligible for a credit card and will get a credit score.

However, the debit card is VISA/MasterCard enabled so it will work everywhere (even online purchases).

Student Accommodation

StudentOne and Scape are some of the reputed (and quality) brands for student accommodation. UniLodge is also a name in the market. StudentOne is mainly in the CBD (the City) and Scape is all over South Bank (with one UniLodge building).

Other alternatives are lease apartments that have two categories:

  • Uber rich Arab Sheikh kids or those from Monaco staying in 222 Brisbane Skytower by Cllix
  • Those who are really tight on budget can search for a granny flat (shared space) in cheapest possible area called Woodridge

There are some student backpacker places in the city, but I don't believe they allow long term residence. They are built like dorms and only God and the person living there knows about the hygiene conditions there.

Student vs "Student"

While it is legally allowed to work part-time (as per conditions stated in your student visa), some students are really not here for studying. They dream of becoming billionaires so they think going to uni is a waste of their precious time. It's their American dream but in the wrong continent!

While there is nothing wrong with side-hustle doing Uber Eats, making sandwiches in Subway or waitering at a restaurant, some students maintain their part-time work as more important than their studies. They are the ones who get poor grades and sometimes get caught at the airport due to abuse of student visa.

Good students make full use of all the facilities provided by university. The library is open 24x7; they work as volunteers for HiQ, are active in university student clubs, use spare time to learn extra skills and go the extra mile.

Social Media Influencer vs Professional Networking

Yes, admit it; your Instagram will be filled with photos of places all over Australia! Some think student visa granted is a tourist visa grant, oblivious to the fact that you need to be eligible for TR (Temporary Residence) afterwards and PR (Permanent Residence) after that.

No one is interested in photos of your matcha drinks or your visit to the beach. But if you really want to be job-ready then participate in business events (seminars, tech talks, symposium, expos), build your LinkedIn networks and not Instagram followers.

Academic Success Tips at QUT

  • While lectures are recorded and available for viewing anytime on Canvas in Echo360, attend lectures in person. Your parents paid for you to study in uni, so better attend face-to-face.
  • Must attend all tutorials in person - the attendance in tutorials is recorded and submitted to government.
  • Try to spend as much time as possible in the uni during semester. Make notes for each class particularly paying interest to the topic or area mentioned by professor.
  • There is no harm in part-time work but legally that's 4 hours per day. You still have the whole day so attend all classes and tutorials.
  • Use the university account (email ID/Windows credentials) to install Office and OneDrive on your personal laptop.
  • Install QUT, Canvas and Rubric App on your mobile.
  • Never procrastinate or leave assignments, projects etc. at the last minute but rather try to do it as soon as it is released.

Academic Integrity Warning: Never use AI or outsource your assignment. The uni uses Turnitin with AI detection tool! Cheaters are reported to the Academic Integrity Council. You cannot outsmart your professors - they too use AI to check your assignments!
